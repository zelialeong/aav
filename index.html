01 — The Problem

The program lives
in people's heads.

The best matches happen because the right person knows both the founder and the mentor. That's not a system — it's a dependency.

⚠

Single point of failure

When the person who knows every mentor leaves or runs out of bandwidth, the program stalls. No documentation survives this.

⚡

Doesn't scale with cohort growth

Manual matching works for 10 startups. At 25+, the mental model of 58 mentors × 25 founders becomes impossible to maintain.

⏱

Reactive, not proactive

Founders ask for help only when they think to. Without a clear picture of what mentors offer, many high-value intros never happen.

👁

Mentor dark matter

Most mentors in the database have zero structured data — no skills, no stage preference, no geography. They're invisible to anyone who wasn't there when they joined.

"If you leave, the mentor program dies. If Sherwin runs out of time, the mentor program dies again. So there needs to be an element of self-matching — something scalable."

— General Partner, Accelerating Asia
